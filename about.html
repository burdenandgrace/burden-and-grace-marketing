About

Built for the man who is tired of carrying.

Not tired of work. Tired of carrying. The weight that doesn't show up on a to-do list — the one you wake up with at 5 a.m. before the alarm.

The why

Most men never say it out loud.

They carry the weight of the job, the weight of the marriage, the weight of being the one everyone else leans on. They carry a failure from ten years ago that still plays at 2 a.m. in the morning. They carry anger they can't explain and shame they can't outrun. They carry it quietly, and they carry it alone.

Burden and Grace is a five-minute morning space for those men. It isn't a program to finish. It isn't a seven-step plan. It isn't a Bible app trying to turn scripture into a streak.

It's a quiet room — before the day consumes you — where one verse and one honest reflection meet you in the thing you've been carrying, and you get to put some of it down.

"Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest."
— Matthew 11:28

That verse is the whole thesis of the app. Everything inside it is built to help a man actually go to the One who promised the rest — not build him a better productivity system.

How we speak

Firm about what's true. Gentle about what you do.

A devotional meets a man in his private morning. The voice matters. So we keep two rules — one for the gospel, one for the application.

Firm

What God has done.

The receipt is nailed to the cross. The case is closed.

These are declarations, not suggestions. A man doesn't need softened gospel — he needs a rock to stand on before he decides what to do next.

Gentle

What you might do.

Here is a step worth trying. Notice what stirs. Follow it if the Spirit confirms.

These are invitations, not commands. You know your marriage, your job, your life. The Spirit convicts. The devotional only points.

Promises, written down

A short list of things we won't do.

An app for tired men should probably keep a few things off the table. These are ours.

—No streaks designed to shame you.
—No algorithm tuning your scroll.
—No ads.
—No journal entry you wrote yesterday being re-writable today.
—No upsells inside a track.
—No "beast mode."
—No hustle language pretending to be discipleship.
—No performance-dressed-up-as-obedience.

Who built this

One man. One idea. One morning at a time.

Burden and Grace was built by a Christian man who needed something that met him at 6 a.m. with a verse, a short reflection, and a place to put down what he was carrying — without turning the whole thing into a leaderboard.

So he built it. For himself, first. Then for other men who were carrying the same kind of thing quietly.

It isn't a parachurch ministry. It isn't a substitute for your local church. It's a five-minute companion — small and honest on purpose, built by someone who needed it too.

The door is open

Five minutes. One verse. Somewhere to put it down.

That's the whole promise. Start with the free ten days. See if it meets you where you actually are.
